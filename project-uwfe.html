Waterloo Formula Electric

Designing the future of electric racing through innovative CAD modeling and mechanical systems optimization

Project Overview

As a member of the Waterloo Formula Electric team, I contributed to the design and development of high-performance electric race cars. My role focused on CAD modeling, mechanical system optimization, and chassis design for competition vehicles.

The team competes in Formula SAE Electric competitions, where we design, build, and race electric vehicles against universities from around the world. This experience provided hands-on engineering practice in a fast-paced, collaborative environment.

My Contributions

Chassis Design & Optimization

Led CAD modeling efforts for the chassis subsystem, focusing on weight reduction while maintaining structural integrity and safety standards.

  • Created detailed 3D models using SolidWorks for chassis components
  • Conducted FEA analysis to optimize material usage and reduce weight by 15%
  • Collaborated with manufacturing team to ensure design feasibility
  • Implemented design changes based on testing feedback and competition requirements

Mechanical Systems Integration

Worked on integrating various mechanical systems within the vehicle architecture.

  • Designed mounting solutions for battery packs and electrical components
  • Created suspension mounting points with adjustable geometry
  • Developed cooling system routing for optimal thermal management
  • Ensured compliance with Formula SAE competition rules and regulations

Documentation & Technical Drawings

  • Produced comprehensive technical drawings with GD&T specifications
  • Created assembly instructions for manufacturing team
  • Maintained design documentation and version control
  • Contributed to competition design reports and presentations
