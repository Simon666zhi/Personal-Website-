Circadian Rhythm Device

A Smart Lighting System for Natural Sleep-Wake Cycle Regulation

üéØ Project Overview

The Circadian Rhythm Device is an intelligent lighting system designed to help regulate natural sleep-wake cycles by mimicking the sun's daily light intensity and color temperature patterns. This device addresses the growing problem of circadian rhythm disruption caused by artificial lighting and screen exposure in modern life.

Developed as a personal wellness technology project, the device automatically adjusts its light output throughout the day to support optimal melatonin production and alertness levels. The system features a custom-designed enclosure, precision LED control, and ambient light sensing capabilities.

System Architecture

The device operates using a time-based control algorithm that calculates optimal color temperature and brightness values based on the current time and user-configured schedule. The ESP32 maintains accurate time synchronization via NTP servers and adjusts light output smoothly throughout the day using high-frequency PWM signals.

The ambient light sensor provides feedback to prevent over-illumination in bright environments while maintaining effectiveness in dark conditions. All settings and schedules are stored in non-volatile memory, ensuring the device maintains functionality even after power cycles.

üîß Design & Development Process

Research & Concept

The project began with research into circadian biology and the effects of light on human physiology. Studies show that exposure to blue-rich light in the evening suppresses melatonin production, while warm, dim lighting promotes natural sleep preparation. This informed the core design requirements for the device.

CAD Design & Enclosure

The enclosure was modeled in SolidWorks with careful attention to several key factors: effective light diffusion for even illumination, heat dissipation for LED longevity, compact form factor for bedside placement, and an aesthetic that complements modern interior design. The housing features a matte finish to reduce glare and a modular design for easy assembly and maintenance.

Electronics & Integration

Electronic design focused on precise PWM control for smooth color temperature transitions, low-power operation for energy efficiency, and reliable WiFi connectivity. The ambient light sensor placement was carefully optimized to avoid interference from the device's own output.

Testing & Validation

  • Light output measured with calibrated lux meter to verify target brightness levels
  • Color temperature validated using spectrometer across full range
  • Long-term thermal testing to ensure safe operating temperatures
  • WiFi reliability tested under various network conditions
  • User experience testing with multiple individuals over several weeks

üí° Key Takeaways

  • User-Centered Design: Initial prototypes were too bright and distracting. User feedback led to refinements in diffuser design and default brightness levels.
  • Heat Management: Early LED configurations generated excessive heat. Adding proper heat sinks and optimizing airflow paths prevented thermal issues.
  • Seamless Integration: The device needed to "disappear" into daily routines. Automatic scheduling and silent operation were essential to achieving this.
  • Software Reliability: Implementing watchdog timers and error handling ensured the device remained operational even during WiFi outages or unexpected conditions.
  • Manufacturing Considerations: Designing for 3D printing required iterative adjustments to wall thickness, support structures, and part tolerances.
